The Age of Fire

— from the First Flame to the Curse —

In the Age of Ancients, the world was unformed, shrouded by fog. A land of gray crags, Archtrees, and Everlasting Dragons. But then there was Fire, and with it came Disparity: Heat and cold, life and death, and of course... Light and Dark. From the Dark, They came, and found the Souls of Lords within the flame... [citation:3]

The First Flame

The primordial spark that introduced disparity to a gray, static reality. Out of its heat, the Lord Souls materialized, laying down the foundational pillars of light, death, and chaotic flame.

The Lords

Gwyn, Nito, the Witch of Izalith, and the Furtive Pygmy seized the immense powers within the Fire. Betrayed by Seath, they crushed the immortal dragons to rule the world [citation:4].

The Fading

As the First Flame wanes, light recedes and the dark takes dominion. Gwyn committed the ultimate sin by sacrifice, attempting to link the fire and cursing humanity to cycles of hollowing.

The Chosen Undead

An outcast brandished with the curse, prophesied to escape the Asylum. Propelled through a dying kingdom, you are left to choose whether to feed the flame or initiate the age of dark.

The Curse of the Undead

The curse binds the Undead to the Darksign — a brand that refuses death but devours humanity. Those who lose their purpose hollow, becoming mindless shells. Only purpose, or the linking of the flame, can stave off the dark.

Covenants

— the factions that shape the fate of Lordran —

Covenants are factions that players can align with, offering unique benefits, items, and multiplayer interactions. Each covenant is deeply rooted in the game's lore and reflects different philosophies and allegiances [citation:4].

Key Items

— the artifacts that define the Undead journey —

The Darksign

A branding representing the curse of the Undead [citation:2]. It ties humanity directly to bonfires, sending the cursed back to safety at the costly sacrifice of accumulated souls and humanity [citation:2].

Humanity

Black sprites found deep inside human bosoms [citation:1]. They define core item discovery rates and resistances [citation:1]. Consuming humanity reverts hollowing but risks total loss upon unexpected death [citation:1].

The Lordvessel

An relic bestowed by Gwynevere, allowing warping between distant bonfires [citation:10]. The vessel is required to gather the Lord Souls to break the sealed gates of the Kiln of the First Flame [citation:3].

Souls

The universal currency and life energy extracted from fallen victims [citation:6]. Required for level progression and weapon ascension, souls represent the burden and struggle of the Undead journey [citation:6].

Estus Flask

The lifeblood of every Undead adventurer. Filled with the warmth of bonfires, this flask restores vitality when all hope seems lost. Its strength grows with each Kindling.

Homeward Bone

A petrified bone imbued with memories of home. When used, teleports the bearer to the last bonfire rested at — a lifeline when venturing too deep into danger.

Titanite Shard

A rare geologic fragment essential for weapon and armor reinforcement. Without these shards, even the mightiest blades remain dull and ineffective against Lordran's horrors.

Covenant of Artorias

A sacred ring bestowed by the knight Artorias. It grants passage through the Abyss — the only protection against the corrosive dark that consumes all who enter without it.

Ring of Favor and Protection

An ancient ring blessed by the goddess Fina. Boosts HP, stamina, and equip load — but shatters if removed. Once worn, there is no going back.

Black Knight Halberd

A massive polearm wielded by the Black Knights. Its sweeping attacks cleave through multiple foes. Favored by strength builds for its devastating reach.

Great Combustion

A powerful pyromancy that erupts a massive burst of flame at close range. The preferred spell of pyromancers who favor aggression over subtlety.

Havel's Ring

Named after Havel the Rock, this ring greatly increases equip load. It allows warriors to wear the heaviest armor while maintaining mobility.

Choose Your Gift

Before walking into the cursed land, select a key starting item. Choose carefully, as these ancient tools can heavily influence your trajectory [citation:5].

The Timeline of Lordran

— from ancient darkness to the age of fire —

Age of Ancients
The World Before Fire
In the beginning, there was only gray. The world was unformed, shrouded by fog. A land of gray crags, Archtrees, and Everlasting Dragons. There was no life, no death, no light, no dark — only the eternal gray.
The First Flame
Fire Awakens Disparity
Then there was Fire, and with it came Disparity. Heat and cold, life and death, and of course... Light and Dark. From the Dark, They came, and found the Souls of Lords within the flame.
The Dragon Wars
Lords Crush the Everlasting Dragons
Gwyn and his knights waged war against the dragons. With Seath's betrayal exposing their weakness to lightning, and Nito's plague of death, the lords crushed the immortal dragons to rule the world.
Age of Fire
The Golden Age of Gods
The Age of Fire began. Gwyn ruled from Anor Londo, the Witch of Izalith practiced her pyromancy, Nito slumbered in death, and the Furtive Pygmy waited in shadow. The world flourished under the gods' reign.
The Fading
The First Flame Wanes
The First Flame began to fade. Light receded and the dark took dominion. Gwyn committed the ultimate sin by sacrificing himself to link the fire, cursing humanity to cycles of hollowing.
The Curse
The Undead Curse Spreads
The Darksign branded humanity, binding them to the bonfires. Those who died returned, but each time they lost more of themselves. The Undead Curse spread across the land, driving men to madness.
The Prophecy
The Chosen Undead Rises
A prophecy was born: an undead, unhollowed, would ring the Bells of Awakening, collect the Lord Souls, and either link the fire or usher in the Age of Dark. Your journey begins in the Northern Undead Asylum.
